## 3. The pitch arc (canonical script)

The **canonical slide-by-slide script** lives in [`PITCH-SLIDES.md`](./PITCH-SLIDES.md). That file is what you memorize and time with a stopwatch.

### Messaging rule: big vision vs wedge (one module vs full ops)

In a **3-minute** pitch you cannot explain the full operations platform (bookings, CRM, maintenance, finance, etc.). If you try, you sound unfocused.

Instead, use a **two-layer story**:

1. **Layer A — the big vision (10–15 seconds total):** "The operations side becomes the rental OS — bookings, fleet, customers, finance — the full stack described in `OPERATIONS.md`."
2. **Layer B — the wedge you ship first (everything else):** "We start with inspections because it's the highest-pain daily workflow and it creates the switching-cost data moat fastest."
3. **Layer C — the demo proves Layer B only:** the video shows marketplace + inspection + PDF. That is intentional.

**One clean sentence to memorize:**

> "The operations platform is intentionally big — it's the rental OS — but we start with the inspection wedge because it's the daily pain that forces usage and creates the data lock-in."

**What not to do:**

- Do not list 9 modules on a slide.
- Do not say "we will build everything" without naming what ships first.
- Do not pretend the product is already the full OS if it isn't — say "roadmap" in one word and move on.

**Q&A is where the full ops map belongs.** If Jānis asks "what's next after inspections?", answer with 3 bullets max: bookings + workflow engine, fleet + maintenance, finance dashboards — then stop.

### If the venue forces fewer slides

Use the **5-slide merge** described at the bottom of [`PITCH-SLIDES.md`](./PITCH-SLIDES.md). Do not maintain a second parallel script here — it drifts out of sync.

## 4. The 30-second demo video (pre-recorded)

In a 3-minute pitch, **never do a live demo.** A 4-second loading screen will torpedo your timing. Pre-record a tight 30-second video and embed it in **Slide 4** of [`PITCH-SLIDES.md`](./PITCH-SLIDES.md).

### Production plan

- **Tool**: OBS, ScreenFlow, or QuickTime + iPhone screen recording
- **Duration**: exactly 30 seconds — measure with a stopwatch
- **Resolution**: 1080p minimum, recorded at the resolution the projector will display
- **Audio**: subtitle-only (no voiceover needed; you narrate live during the slide). Music optional, low volume.
- **No mouse cursor visible** when possible. Touch interactions on phone look better.
- **Subtitled labels** appear at key moments to orient the viewer

### The 30-second cut

| Time | What's on screen | Optional subtitle |
|---|---|---|
| 0–4s | Marketplace homepage zooming into "Riga" city page | "60 local rentals across the Baltics" |
| 4–8s | Scroll through Riga listings, click BalticCarRent | "Customers find local rentals" |
| 8–12s | Profile page with WhatsApp / call / email buttons. Tap WhatsApp. | "Direct contact, no middleman" |
| 12–14s | Cut to operator dashboard logged in (different device frame) | "Operator side" |
| 14–17s | Tap "Start pickup inspection" | "Pickup inspection" |
| 17–22s | Photo capture × 3, swipe through | "Photo + damage tag" |
| 22–25s | Tap to mark a damage on silhouette | |
| 25–28s | Tap "Submit" → "Locked" confirmation | "Locked. Time-stamped." |
| 28–30s | PDF report flips into view, hold | "Dispute-proof PDF" |

### Hard rules for the video

1. **Real product, real data.** No mockups. No Figma. The marketplace must be the actual carrentdesk.com.
2. **Real design-partner content.** BalticCarRent's profile must show real photos, real description, real fleet info.
3. **A real inspection.** The damage marked must be from an actual inspection done by a design partner, not a fake demo.
4. **No transitions or motion graphics** beyond simple fades. Looks gimmicky.
5. **Re-record after every product change** in the final 2 weeks. The video must reflect the current product on June 12.

### Live narration during the video

While the video plays, you say (out loud, on stage):

> "Sixty real rentals across three capitals. Customers find them, call them directly, no booking middleman. On the operator side we start with the inspection wedge — photos, mark damage, submit, locked, time-stamped PDF. This is the daily pain that creates the switching-cost moat first; the rest of the rental OS follows on the roadmap."

Time it to end exactly when the video ends. Practice this 30 times.

## 10. Q&A — 2 minutes, expect 4–6 questions

Each answer = **15–20 seconds maximum.** No rambling. End every answer cleanly so the next question can come fast.

### Universal questions (any judge could ask)

**Q: How is this different from Booking.com / Turo / Getaround?**
A: We're not transactional. We're a discovery directory for **local independent rentals** — the ones Booking doesn't list and Turo doesn't fit. The moat is the operations platform.

**Q: What stops Sixt or Booking from copying this?**
A: They serve the top 20% of the market. We serve the bottom 80% — local independents Sixt actively excludes. And the moat is daily relationships, not features. They can't replicate sitting in a parking lot doing inspections with the founder.

**Q: How do you make money?**
A: Marketplace listing is free forever — that's the hook. Operators pay €[X]/month for the ops platform. Founding partners get 50% off forever; new sign-ups pay full.

**Q: How big is the Baltic market?**
A: ~[NUMBER] rental companies across LV/EE/LT. At €[X]/month, that's a €[X]M SaaS market on its own — before adjacent markets like Poland and Czech Republic.

**Q: Why won't operators just stay on Excel?**
A: They will, until inspection disputes cost them money. We replace WhatsApp damage arguments with PDF proof. Three of my competitors agreed to be design partners specifically for this.

**Q: How do you scale onboarding?**
A: First 200 are manual outbound — that's the right speed for the segment. After that, the marketplace network ("you're already listed → claim it") scales itself.

**Q: What's your unfair advantage?**
A: I run BalticCarRent in Riga. I know which 30 owners to call tomorrow. I'm building the system I needed for myself.

**Q: What if you don't raise?**
A: Slower path. The product runs either way. With €50K I reach 50 paying operators in 12 months. Without, 24 months.

### If Māris asks (Shopify-economics lens)

**Q: What's your CAC, LTV, churn assumption?**
A: At this stage projections only. Manual outbound CAC ~€50 (founder's own time). LTV assumption: €X × 24 months retention = €X. Churn target: under 5%/month — defensible because of accumulated inspection history, which is the switching cost.

**Q: Why isn't this a feature inside an existing rental software?**
A: Existing rental software solves bookings, not operations pain. Inspections are a feature gap they've ignored for 15 years. We're not adding to their stack — we're replacing it from the bottom up, starting with the most painful daily workflow.

**Q: What's the path to €1M ARR?**
A: 200 paying operators × €50/month = €120K MRR = €1.4M ARR. With the network effect at ~50 operators per Baltic capital, this is reachable in 24 months without leaving the Baltics. Faster with funding.

### If Jānis asks (marketplace / supply-side lens)

**Q: How are you solving the cold-start problem?**
A: The same way Printify did — supply first. We're seeding with 60 unclaimed listings across Riga, Tallinn, Vilnius, then converting them into active operators via outbound. Three are already converted. The marketplace looks real on day one.

**Q: How do you build network effects?**
A: Three layers. (1) More operators → richer marketplace → more customer traffic → more value per operator. (2) Inspection data accumulating per operator increases switching cost. (3) Visibility ranking among local competitors creates competitive pressure to stay active.

**Q: Why the Baltics first?**
A: I know the market, the language, the operators personally. The Baltics are small enough to dominate quickly and large enough to validate. Once 50 operators are paying across 3 capitals, the same playbook scales to Poland, Czech Republic, then DACH region.

**Q: What's the next vertical?**
A: Boats. Same dynamics — fragmented local operators, no shared platform, daily inspection pain, big-brand pressure (Boataround). But that's post-€1M-ARR. We're focused.

### If Anna asks (program leader, looking for coachable founders)

**Q: What's the biggest risk in this plan?**
A: Operator activation lag. They sign up, don't use it daily, churn. Mitigation: in-person onboarding with each design partner, weekly feedback calls, ruthless focus on the inspection tool until it's used daily without thinking.

**Q: What feedback have you ignored?**
A: I was told to start with bookings, not inspections. I ignored it because bookings is what dozens of failed rental SaaS companies have built. Inspections is where the daily pain is, and where the moat is.
