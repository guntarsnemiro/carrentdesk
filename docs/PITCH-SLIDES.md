# CarRentDesk — Pitch Slides Draft (Text Only)

> Text-only slide draft for the June 12 Startschool graduation pitch.
> Format target: **3:00 minutes total**, 5–7 slides, 2:00 minutes Q&A separate.
> See [`PITCH.md`](./PITCH.md) for strategy and [`PITCH.md` §4](./PITCH.md) for the 30-second demo video shot list.

---

## How to use this document

1. **Each slide has 4 fields**: title, on-slide text, what you say live, and time budget.
2. **Slide text is final on-screen content.** Keep it short — judges read in 1–2 seconds, then look back at you.
3. **What you say live is your spoken script.** Memorize it. Practice with stopwatch.
4. **Time budgets sum to 180 seconds.** If you go long on one slide, cut from another.
5. **Do not add images, charts, or motion graphics yet.** Lock the words first; visuals come after.

---

This version uses 7 slides. Each slide is short, which lets you pause for emphasis. Collapse to 5 slides only if the venue insists.

### Slide 2 — The Two Failure Modes

**On-slide text:**

```
Local rentals die in two ways:

Years 1–3:  Not enough growth.
            Big chains take all the visibility.
            They run out of money.

Years 5–8:  Owner overload.
            WhatsApp + Excel + paper damage forms.
            They burn out, sell, or quit.
```

**What you say live (30 seconds):**

> "First three years: they can't grow fast enough. Sixt and Booking own the airport, own Google, own the brand recognition. A new local rental can't be found. They run out of money before reaching scale.
>
> Years five to eight: the survivors get crushed by their own operations. WhatsApp messages, Excel sheets, paper damage forms. The owner is working seven days a week. They quit, sell, or burn out.
>
> Both failures are solvable. Nobody is solving them."

**Time budget:** 30 seconds

**Memorable line:** "Years one to three they fail from no growth. Years five to eight they quit from overload."

### Slide 3 — The Solution

**On-slide text:**

```
CarRentDesk = two products, one platform.

MARKETPLACE  →  solves the growth problem (years 1–3)
              Free directory of local rentals.
              Customers contact rentals directly.

OPERATIONS    →  solves the overload problem (years 5–8)
              Starts with inspections (daily pain + proof).
              Expands into the full rental OS (roadmap).

The same operator pays for both as they grow.
```

**What you say live (25 seconds):**

> "CarRentDesk is two products on one platform. The marketplace solves the growth problem — a free directory that brings local rentals customers without a Sixt-sized marketing budget.
>
> The operations side becomes the rental OS over time — bookings, fleet, customers, finance — but we start with inspections because it's the daily pain: photos, damage tags, locked PDF. That's the switching-cost moat. The rest follows once we're embedded daily.
>
> Marketplace gets them in. Operations keeps them locked in."

**Time budget:** 25 seconds

**Memorable line:** "Marketplace gets them in. Operations keeps them locked in."

## Speaker notes — global rules

1. **No reading the slides.** Every word on screen must already be in your head; the slide is for the audience, not for you.
2. **Pause after memorable lines.** "Fifteen years." [pause] "I've watched..." Pauses signal confidence.
3. **Eye contact 80% of the time.** Glance at the slide only briefly to advance.
4. **Stand still.** Don't pace. Hands at your sides or one hand on the slide remote.
5. **Don't apologize.** No "I know I'm running out of time" or "this is just a quick demo." Confidence carries.
6. **Land the closing line clean.** "Now I'm building it for everyone like me." [pause] [smile] "Questions?"

### Pitch messaging rule (3 minutes)

- **Name the full ops vision in one breath** ("rental OS over time").
- **Prove only the wedge in the demo** (inspections + PDF).
- **Save the module tour for Q&A** (3 bullets max).

## Slide design principles (for the visual designer or for you)

When you design the actual deck (week 5 or 6):

- **Black background or white background** — pick one, never mix
- **One sans-serif font.** Inter, Geist, or Söhne. Nothing else.
- **Maximum 5 lines of text per slide.** Fewer is better.
- **No bullet characters** — use line breaks instead. Bullets feel corporate.
- **One accent color** for emphasis (your brand blue from `tailwind.config`)
- **Slide numbers off** for a 7-slide pitch
- **No logos in the corner** — distracts from content
- **Real product screenshots only** if you add visuals. No mockups.
